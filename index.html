Country Warming Explorer (CMIP6)

This visualization uses the CMIP6 climate model projections to show how much warmer the top 5 most polluted countries will become under different future emissions scenarios/predictions (SSP1–2.6 through SSP5–8.5). The difference is computed relative to the selected countries model-estimated 1850–1900 pre-industrial temperature.

The Paris Agreement is an international climate treaty adopted in 2015, in which almost every country agreed to limit global warming this century to well below 2 °C above pre-industrial levels and to aim to limit global warming to 1.5 °C.

SSP stands for Shared Socioeconomic Pathways, which are future world scenarios used in climate research. Each SSP is a scenario that represents a different combination of population growth, technology development, policy choices, and greenhosue gas emissions. Lower-emission pathways like SSP1–2.6 assume strong global cooperation and rapid decarbonization, while high-emission pathways such as SSP5–8.5 assume continued fossil fuel use and limited climate policy.

Note: A temperature difference measures how much warmer a given year is compared to the 1850–1900 average for the selected countries.

For example: If in 2027 the annual mean temperature in the United States is predicted to be 13 °C and the 1850–1900 average in the United States is 12 °C, then the difference is 1 °C.

The shaded bands and dashed lines at 1.5 °C and 2.0 °C mark the Paris Agreement warming goals.
