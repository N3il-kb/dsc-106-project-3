Country Warming Explorer (CMIP6)

This visualization uses CMIP6 climate model projections to show how much warmer the selected countries become under different future emissions scenarios (SSP1–2.6 through SSP5–8.5). Anomalies are computed relative to the selected countries model-estimated 1850–1900 pre-industrial temperature. The shaded bands show the Paris Agreement’s 1.5 °C and 2.0 °C temperature goals.

5

Note: A temperature anomaly measures how much warmer a given year is compared to the 1850–1900 average for the selected countries.

For example: If in 2027 the annual mean temperature in the United States is predicted to be 13 °C and the 1850–1900 average in the United States is 12 °C, then the anomaly is 1 °C.

The shaded bands and dashed lines at 1.5 °C and 2.0 °C mark the Paris Agreement warming goals.
